Why Do People Seek Approval?

People seek approval for two main reasons:


They lack self-confidence and self-esteem, and thus look for other people to give it to them. People who lack confidence tend to seek permission for their actions. This means that they value others’ opinions more than their own. Remember, it’s not self-confidence when someone else gives it to you!


They want to be liked by everyone. When a person feels the need to be liked by everyone, it affects all of their actions. It can get so pervasive that it impacts their behavior even when they’re alone, because they’ll imagine people or a certain someone watching their every move. The solution for this is something I call rebellion practice, which is as fun as it sounds.
